    • History:
    The Roman Empire was divided in half in the fourth century.
    The eastern half, which we now refer to as the Byzantine Empire, included Egypt.
    Nonetheless, the Arabs took control of it in the seventh century. In 639, the Arabs invaded Egypt, and by 642, they controlled the whole country.

    It joined the Islamic Empire under Baghdad's control. Egypt did became independent in 868 under the leadership of Ahmed Ibn Tulun, but this freedom did not survive long.
    With the murder of his son and heir, Baghdad's power was reinstated.
    The Tunisian Fatimids then conquered Egypt in 969. Up until 1171, Egypt was controlled by the Fatimids, who built Cairo, the nation's capital.

    Tourism:

    As we all know Egpyt is famous for the Pryramids and millions of people all across the world come to visit and see the Pharoh's body.

    According to a report released by the Central Bank of Egypt (CBE), Egypt's tourist revenue increased by 43.5 % to a record-breaking 4.1 billion US dollars in the first quarter of the financial year 2022-2023,
    which runs from July to September of last year.

    According to the statement, the number of visitors travelling to Egypt from July through September of last year jumped by 52.2 percent to 3.4 million, and the number of nights they stayed increased by 47.1 percent to 43.6 million.
